Summary

To say that Christopher Nolan's THE DARK KNIGHT is the best comic book adaptation does the film a bit... more
To say that Christopher Nolan's THE DARK KNIGHT is the best comic book adaptation does the film a bit of a disservice. It may be a Batman film, but this isn't a kid-friendly action movie full of the bams and pows of the original series. This is a pitch-black thriller with enough drama and tragedy to please even William Shakespeare, but it will certainly keep both comics fans and uninitiated audiences equally happy. THE DARK KNIGHT starts in the wake of BATMAN BEGINS: with the appearance of Batman (Christian Bale), Gotham City's criminal underworld is unnerved. They're also plagued by the new D.A., Harvey Dent (Aaron Eckhart), who, in his quest for justice, remains above the city's corruption. Enter a new villain, the Joker (Heath Ledger), who wants to unite the criminals for a common purpose: to kill Batman.

THE DARK KNIGHT is one of the most hyped movies to date, and a lesser film would be crushed under the weight of all that expectation. Some of the publicity stems from the early death of Ledger, who turns in an excellent performance. He provides moments of humor, but this Joker is terrifying, sharing more with classic villains such as Hannibal Lechter than with his comic book predecessors. Eckhart is equally good as Dent, and Maggie Gyllenhaal deserves praise for taking over the role of Rachel Dawes from Katie Holmes. Though there's more emphasis on plot and character development than in most comic book adaptations, that doesn't mean Nolan has skimped on any of the action sequences. Each set piece is done perfectly, leaving the audience breathless. THE DARK KNIGHT is filmmaking at its best; its subject matter may be dark and depressing, but it's tough not to feel exhilarated by its artistry when the credits begin to roll. less

This movie is ridiculous, no doubt about it. But the point people sometimes seem to miss is that it is meant to be ridiculous. Sure, the movie is painted as a dark, gritty, realistic world, but, it really isnt. It is a movie about a man dressed up as a bat, beating up bad guys, and chasing a man dressed as a clown. Its glorious. Comicbooks, have, for a long time, presented themselves as gritty, or dark, and The Dark Knight takes that idea and puts it on film. Batman Begins, in a way, redefined what a comicbook movie should be, but The Dark Knight redefined what a comicbook movie could be. Christopher Nolan saw that Batman taps into the archetypes of the classic film-noir, and decided to put that spin on it - to the extreme. Everything in this movie is extreme: the action, the acting, the dialogue, the story, the characters, everything is extreme and very dramatic, almost to the point of being goofy. Well, it is goofy, seriously, just look at the scenes where Batman is talking with Jim Gordon about the fate of Gotham City, its ridiculous, absolutely ridiculous, and yet it takes itself very seriously. I love it. It is as if the comicbook just leapt from the page to the screen. Really, besides a few minor pacing issues, this film is nearly perfect: Easily the best film from 2008. Yeah, Im looking at you Slumdog Millionaire.
